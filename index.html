Credit Card Checkout

For this personal project, I wanted to bring the physical card transaction experience to the digital realm.

Here were the initial sketches. My thoughts on the animation going into this project was that it would be most useful as a credit card checkout confirmation screen. As a result, for this mockup I focused on the screens that would function as such.

I then transitioned into Adobe XD after I felt confident in the concept and visual direction. I first laid out the elements in grayscale to focus on value and visual hierarchy. This step was followed by color application based on both light and dark pallettes. I ultimately chose the dark theme for the mockup.

The assets were then exported and carried into Adobe After Effects. Using an iPhone X mockup, the elements were animated to carry out the intended user controlled movements of the pieces.

SPB Landing Page Redesign

This personal project aimed to give the Washington University Social Programming Board's website a look that places emphasis on the bi-annual concert on campus, WILD.

The goals of this project focused on developing good visual design practices for the screen. Here I'm really thinking about design systems that are easy to manage (due to the need to update events) and better representation of the Social Programming Board. Because of these aspects I wanted to make the events bolder and brought to the forefront of the landing page.

Tell Me How: Where Does Natural Gas Come From?

This image-focused project was for Word & Image II at Washington University. The challenge for this project was to construct a book using nothing but images that communicated an immutable story. The story I chose delt with a basic explanation about where natural gas came from.

To create the forms for this piece, I drew out these scenes using dirt and illuminated them from below using a lightbox. I used a camera to capture the layout from above imported them into illustrator.

"The blue flame under the pot on your stove comes from burning gas."

"This gas was made in the earth."

"People drill to find this natural gas. They find it deep down in pockets underground."

"Huge pipes are laid to carry the natural gas away. The pipes run for hundreds and hundreds of miles, often over many states. Smaller pipes branch out to streets, to homes, to stoves."

"Other forms of burnable gas, like propane, are manufactured in processing plants"
