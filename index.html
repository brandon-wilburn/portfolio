Interface | Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe After Effects

Credit Card Checkout

For this personal project, I wanted to focus on intergrating the physical experience of checking out with a credit card.


Here were the initial sketches of how I was thinking the interface could be. I began thinking this animation would be most useful as a credit card checkout confirmation screen. As a result, I focused on the screens that would function as such.


I then transitioned into Adobe XD after I felt confident in the concept and visual direction. I first laid out the elements in grayscale to focus on value and visual hierarchy. This step was followed by color application based on both light and dark pallettes. I ultimately chose the dark theme for the mockup.



The assets were then exported and carried into Adobe After Effects. Using an iPhone X mockup, the elements were then animated to carryout the intended user controlled movements of the pieces.

SPB Landing Page Redesign

This personal project aimed to give the Washington University Social Programming Board's website a look that places emphasis on the bi-annual concert on campus, WILD.



The goals of this project focused on developing good visual design practices for the screen. Here I'm really thinking about design systems that are easy to manage, due to the need to update events, and better representation of the Social Programming Board. Because of these aspects, when thinking about what could be better about the current SPB website, I wanted to make the events bolder and brought to the forefront of the splash page.

Student Life: WU: In Focus

WU: in Focus is a journalistic piece produced by Student Life, the Washington University Independent Newspaper, in November of 2018. The infographic in the middle of the issue was our main focus.

2D Design | Dirt, Photo, Adobe Illustrator

Tell Me How: Where Does Natural Gas Come From?

This image-focused project was for Word & Image II at Washington University. The challenge for this project was to construct a book using nothing but images that communicated an immutable story. The story I chose delt with a basic explanation about where natural gas came from.



To create the forms for this piece, I drew out these scenes using dirt and illuminated it from below using a lightbox. I used a camera to then capture the layout from above where it was then taken into illustrator.
