Tell Me How.

Illustration only book describing where natural gas comes from and how it is used.

Class

2018

Illustration

Dirt, Photography, Illustrator

The Details.

This image-focused project was for Word & Image II at Washington University. The challenge for this project was to construct a book using nothing but images that communicated an immutable story. The story I chose dealt with a basic explanation about where natural gas comes from. To create these compositions, I drew out these scenes using dirt and illuminated them from below using a lightbox. I then used a camera to capture the layout from above and imported the photos into illustrator.

"The blue flame under the pot on your stove comes from burning gas."

"This gas was made in the earth."

"People drill to find this natural gas. They find it deep down in pockets underground."

"Huge pipes are laid to carry the natural gas away. The pipes run for hundreds and hundreds of miles, often over many states. Smaller pipes branch out to streets, to homes, to stoves."

"Other forms of burnable gas, like propane, are manufactured in processing plants"
